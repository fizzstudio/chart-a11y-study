Fizz Studio: Assessing Chart Accessibility

This small test environment is intended to be used to help evaluate access barriers in online, interactive charts and graphs. We are evaluating a series of interactive charts and graphs with various assistive technologies and native users.

These environments are set up so that participants can help us evaluate the usability of these charts and graphs, they are not intended to test the individual abilities of our participants.

The following section is where our test chart will render. These tests may be changed by choosing an option in the previous Environments nav menu.

When we are ready to begin evaluating, please select First Chart in the previous Environments menu.

Tasks:

  1. Which product had the lowest sales, and what was the value?
  2. Which product had the highest sales, and what region was that in?
  3. What is the range between the highest sales and the lowest sales?

Tasks:

  1. What is the highest value in both lines?
  2. What is the trend of each line?
  3. At what month did one line overtake the other?

Tasks:

  1. What type of chart is this? What does it show?
  2. What is the company with the lowest value?
  3. What is the total market share in dollars of all companies based in Mexico?

Tasks:

  1. How many people have heights between 159.5 and 169.5 centimeters ?
  2. How many people have heights less than 159.5 centimeters ?
  3. How many people are in the most common range of heights, and what is that range?

Tasks:

  1. What type of chart is this? What does it show?
  2. How many absences did the student with the highest GPA have?
  3. Is there a positive or negative correlation between the number of absences of a student and their GPA?

Tasks:

  1. What kind of chart is this?
  2. How many regions are there, and how many cells in each region?
  3. Select the cell with the highest value. What are the details of this cell?

Tasks:

  1. Find the secondary course with the most students. What is the subject?
  2. Which secondary course is most popular after studying science?
  3. Can you identify any secondary course attended by both English and Science students?
